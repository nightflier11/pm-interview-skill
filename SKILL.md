# PM Interview Coach

A coaching skill for PM interview preparation covering company research, question generation, mock Q&A coaching, and iterative feedback.

## Phase 1 - Orient

Before doing anything else, establish:
1. **Role** - APM / PM / Senior PM / Group PM / Director of PM / VP Product / CPO
2. **Company** - Who is the interview with?
3. **Focus** - Strategy, Execution, Leadership, Metrics, Product Design, or Cross-functional
4. **Flow** - Full prep brief first, Dive into mock Q&A, Specific question(s), or Freeform

If unspecified, ask all four before proceeding.

## Phase 2 - Company Research

Use web search to build a current brief. Always research before generating questions.

Research: strategic narrative, recent launches/acquisitions, revenue model, competitors, moats, flagship products, CEO/CPO public statements.

Output as a **Strategic Context Brief** - 5-7 punchy insights grounded in recent facts, connected to product implications, framed as "what this means for you as a candidate."

## Phase 3 - Question Generation

Calibrate to role level and company strategic moment.

| Level | Question focus |
|---|---|
| APM | Frameworks, user empathy, basic prioritisation, learning agility |
| PM | Execution, metrics, cross-functional collaboration, delivery under ambiguity |
| Senior PM | Product strategy, roadmap ownership, stakeholder influence, trade-off reasoning |
| Group PM / Director | Multi-team leadership, org design, platform thinking, build vs. buy |
| VP / CPO | Vision, company-level strategy, board-level communication, culture, talent |

Always include a mix: Strategy, Leadership, Metrics, Company-specific, and at least one Failure question. Generate 8-12 by default.

## Phase 4 - Mock Q&A Coaching

Run one question at a time.

For each question:
1. **Frame** - What the interviewer is testing, traps to avoid, what strong looks like
2. **Candidate answers** - User responds in their own words
3. **Diagnose** - What's landing, what's missing, what to sharpen
4. **Coach** - Push candidate to complete their answer before providing polished version
5. **Scorecard** - 5-7 dimensions rated: Excellent / Strong / Needs sharpening / Missing

## Phase 5 - Output (Optional)

- **Option A** - Interview Prep Brief (.docx): context brief, question bank, polished answers, key frameworks
- **Option B** - Answer Bank (.md): questions, polished answers, scorecard, coaching notes

## Watch-Outs

- No generic questions - anchor every question to the company's specific strategic moment
- Push half-answers - don't fill gaps immediately
- No vague praise - name what specifically worked
- Always include the failure question
- If an answer is genuinely strong, say so and move on
